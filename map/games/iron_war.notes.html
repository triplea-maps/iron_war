Introduction:
It is the year 1940. Germany has expanded eastward and is still on the offensive. All Axis nations are now at war with all Allied nations. All eyes are on Germany - What will their next move be?



Win Conditions:
Total Victory is obtained by an alliance controlling 20 of the 30 VCs at the end of a round.

Economic Victory is obtained by an alliance controlling 650 PUs at the end of a round.



Player Relations:
All Axis players are at war with all Allied players. This map features no diplomacy or possibility to change player relations. Besides the controllable Axis and Allied nations, there are three passive neutral groups:
  • True-Neutral units can be attacked by both Axis and Allies.
    •
  • Pro-Axis-Neutral units can be attacked by Allies and moved through by Axis.
    •
  • Pro-Allied-Neutral units can be attacked by Axis and moved through by Allies.
    •

    Allied Colonies:
    The colonies have their territories spread across the world map. Some of these territories may fall early in the war if the colonies are not supported financially by the larger Allied powers.
    • British-Colonies have factories in South America, Nigeria and Egypt.
      •
    • French-Colonies have factories in South America, French India and French Indochina.

    Rules and Setting:
    This map uses 10-sided dice.

    There are two alliances, 8 Axis vs. 12 Allied.

    Order of play is as listed in the Economy and Stats tabs.

    Some nations can financially support minor nations, as long as these minor nations have a Factory on the map.

    Battleships and Carriers have 2 Hitpoints and are repaired at start of player turns.

    Canals are controlled by single territories marked with boom barriers.

    Factories and Synthetic-Fuel are destroyed during capture.

    Factories can only be placed on 5+ PU value territories.

    Factories can not be placed in newly captured territories.

    Factories have a production capacity of 5 units, but single units may also be placed at Rally-Points.

    Factories have 5 HP, can be strategically bombarded, damaged and lowered in production capacity. Rally-Points cannot be bombarded, but they can be taken over by the enemy.

    Purchased units do not need to be placed in the same round as they are bought.

    It is possible to purchase and place new aircraft directly on newly placed carriers.

    It is possible to escort and intercept Bombers.

    Air battles can only last two rounds.

    Defending suicide units and munitions do not fire if attacked, but they may be taken as casualties. This is true for Kamikaze-Planes, Nuclear-Bombs, V1- and V2-Rockets.

    Anti-Air and Cruisers have an extra special attack every round, with a 10 % hit chance, against aircraft.

    Dive-Bombers have an extra special attack every round, with a 10 % hit chance, against land vehicles and surface ships.

    Tank-Destroyers have an extra special attack every round, with a 10 % hit chance, against land vehicles.



    Options and Requirements:
    Recommended resolution: 1920 x 1080. (Because of the purchase screen size.)

    Music can be turned off by unchecking: Game - Sound Options - Start of Your Turn Control.

    This map is compatible with Fast and Hard AI, not Easy AI.


    Resources:
    PUs are used for all unit purchases. Territories worth 1-4 PUs have silver markers. Territories worth 5+ PUs have golden markers and are able to hold a Factory.

    Allied Convoy Zones provide PUs for many Allied nations as long as they are under control.

    Iron is used to make armor and is required to produce ships and land vehicles.

    Fuel is used by ships, land vehicles and planes to move. 1 unit moving to an adjacent territory consumes 1 Fuel. Planes are an exception to this rule, as they always consume Fuel equal to half of their full potential movement. Note that all Synthetic-Fuel production is destroyed during capture, unlike regular fuel resources.

    Colonial Recruitment (CR) comes from controlling specific territories and is used to purchase colonial infantry units. Several Allied nations plus Italy can make use of this resource.

    Schutzstaffel Recruitment (SS) comes from Axis controlling specific territories and is used to purchase SS infantry and tank units. All SS recruitment is done by Germany.

    Kamikaze Pilots are recruited by Japan in Japan, but this cannot be done before round 9 (year 1944).

    Soviet Officials are created by Commissariats. Officials are used to create Commissars who can inspire USSR Infantry during battle.

    Axis Submarine-Pens continuously create new Submarines throughout the war. Pens also enable Submarines to launch long range attacks and therefore represent a constant threat to Allied fleets until captured and destroyed.

    Allied Airfields continuously create new Allied Air-Transports throughout the war. Airfields will continue to function until they are captured and destroyed by the Axis.

    Some resource deposits around the world are claimed by specific nations from game start. Friendly nations will automatically hand over these resources if captured from enemy forces.


    Timeline and Events:

    Early 1940 (Round 1)
    Germany has just expanded eastward and is still on the offensive.

    Late 1940 (Round 2)
    Finland gains access to German military equipment.

    Early 1941 (Round 3)
    Balkan states gain access to German military equipment.

    Late 1941 (Round 4)
    China gains access to USA military equipment.

    Early 1942 (Round 5)
    Brazil gains access to USA military equipment.

    Late 1942 (Round 6)
    Several nations develop the Heavy-Tank.

    Early 1943 (Round 7)
    Several nations develop the Jet-Fighter.

    Late 1943 (Round 8)
    Germany develops the V1-Rocket.

    Early 1944 (Round 9)
    Japan can make use of Kamikaze-Planes.

    Late 1944 (Round 10)
    Germany develops the V2-Rocket.

    Early 1945 (Round 11)
    USA develops the Nuclear-Bomb.

    Late 1945 (Round 12)
    Germany develops the Nuclear-Bomb.

    Early 1946 (Round 13)
    USSR develops the Nuclear-Bomb.

    Late 1946 (Round 14)
    Japan develops the Nuclear-Bomb.

    Early 1947 (Round 15)
    Britain develops the Nuclear-Bomb.
